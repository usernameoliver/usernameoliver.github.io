Outlier Detection for Clinical Monitoring and Alerting

by Milos Hauskrecht

This talk comes from the DBMI seminar

Medical errors is the third leading cause of death. We need better solutions to prevent/reduce occurrence of adverse events and medical errors.

Dr. Hauskrecht gave a talk on outlier detection in clinical settings. The approach is to utilize previously collected patient data to detect unsual actions -- P(a|x)

The state the patient is represented by a feature vector using a time series of lab feature mappings. Another highlight is the relax of time window; Missing data handled explicitly by classification models; In order to build better model in real time, NOMA.AI platform is constructed.

This talk is an early exploration of patient state representation by summarizing multiple time series and shed light on low dimensional patient state representations
