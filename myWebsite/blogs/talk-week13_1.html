Virtually Indistinguishable Digital Doubles

by Paul Munro

In this talk, Professor Munro vividly visualized the hidden layers of a neural network. He started with the hydrogen atom model of neural nets -- the XOR function of a perceptron. By varying the weights and biases, the network warped the input space to a collaped non-leinear manifold of lower dimensionality.

This talk is very interesting in terms of its idea of revealing the mechanism by building from the most basic element -- XOR funtion. It also reminds me of another paper which attempts to fit the data with the least number of parameters using a neural network. This could be a future direction on the generalizability of deep neural networks.

This talk comes from the ISP Lunch seminar sponsored by PITT
